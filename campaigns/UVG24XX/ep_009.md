## Banana seller (other perspective)

[Pilixa](../../directory/Atles/Pilixa.md), [Kur Barista](../../directory/Atles/Barista.md) and [Cianur](../../directory/Atles/Cianur.md) travel with the large [Atles Caravan](../../directory/Atles/index.md).
They are three days from [The Last Serai](../../directory/LastSerai/index.md).
They see a [metallic stepped tower](../../directory/LastSerai/IgnoredTower.md), glinting in the daylight, glowing a ghostly, coppery green by night.
Two days out, they smell it: soft and seductive like cocoa.

From a hill they see the scene of the [exploding banana seller](ep_008.md).
The protection necroamblers quickly state the obvious:

> *"No one's ever seen a banana boy explode before."*

They befriend them and share business advice.

A day out of their destination, they hear [it](../../directory/LastSerai/Harmonium.md).
Drumming out a rhythmless, rumbling staccato.

## Check-in and visas

Closing in on the tower, you see buildings, like hunched old men, clustered in the lee of a cinder dune.
Around the tower, a circle of gentle dust floats in a massive static charge.
Nothing living grows within that circle.

An employee of the Inspectorate receives them.
The group is explained the bureaucracy of entering the Last Serai.
They also learn that the Porcelain Princes and the Spectrum Satraps both have a hold in this location.
Their visas are signed and stamped.

Inside the Last Serai, the three caravaneers find accommodations to their designated visa types and resources.
Kur stays in the diplomats' rooms, while the others stay in cheaper options: with the slaves.
The trio conducts some market research on selling lavender pouches and investigates locations nearby.
None of them with extreme success.

## Storm

Halfway through the week, a bad cinder storm kicks in, while a rat infestation forces Pilixa and Cianur to be outside.
They are struck by sharp debris that went flying from the dust dunes.
Both are severely wounded.
Kur, in his diplomatic room, develops a rash from the dryness.
Some moisturisers make it better later in the day.

## Transmission

On the last day of the week, a sudden static alert begins.
People talk of the Ignored Tower ramping up its broadcast.
Everyone is paused on their run, seeing and hearing static.

A minute or so later, the static repeats for Cianur.
Kur also sees shadows of spirits, and garbled voices echo through his head.
He feels he will be haunted by them.

Pilixa takes the worst.
Their rings turn into an anvil.
Their hand is severely harmed by it, and a quick surgery is done on the spot.
If it were not for Kur, hours later, applying his vet kit to Pilixa, she might not have made it.
