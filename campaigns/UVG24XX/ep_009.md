## Banana seller (other perspective)

[Pilixa](../../directory/Atles/Pilixa.md), [Kur Barista](../../directory/Atles/Barista.md) and [Cianur](../../directory/Atles/Cianur.md) travel with the large [Atles Caravan](../../directory/Atles/index.md).
Three days out from [The Last Serai](../../directory/LastSerai/index.md).
They see a [metallic stepped tower](../../directory/LastSerai/IgnoredTower.md), glinting in the daylight, glowing a ghostly, coppery green by night.
Two days out, they smell it: soft and seductive like cocoa.

From a hill, they witness the [exploding banana seller](ep_008.md).
The protection necroamblers quickly state the obvious:

> *"No one's ever seen a banana boy explode before."*

They befriend them and share business advice.

A day out, they hear [it](../../directory/LastSerai/Harmonium.md).
Drumming out a rhythmless, rumbling staccato.

## Check-in and visas

Closing in on the tower, buildings cluster in the lee of a cinder dune like hunched, dusty old men.
Around the tower, a circle of gentle dust floats in a massive static charge.
Nothing living grows within that circle.

A fat employee of the Inspectorate receives them.
The group is subjected to the arcane bureaucracy of entering the Last Serai.
They learn the Porcelain Princes and the Spectrum Satraps both have their claws in this location.
Visas are signed, stamped, and begrudgingly issued.

Inside, the caravaneers find accommodations suited to their visa types and dwindling resources.
Kur claims the diplomats' suites.
The others take the cheaper option: bunking with the slaves.
The trio conducts market research on selling lavender pouches and investigates nearby locations.
None of it yields extreme success.

## Storm

Halfway through the week, a bad cinder storm kicks in.
A rat infestation forces Pilixa and Cianur outside into the howling grit.
They are struck by sharp debris flying from the dust dunes.
Both are severely wounded.
Kur, safe in his diplomatic room, develops a mild rash from the dryness.
A dab of moisturiser resolves it later in the day.

## Transmission

On the last day of the week, a sudden static alert begins.
Whispers say the Ignored Tower is ramping up its broadcast.
Everyone pauses in their tracks, seeing and hearing the static wash over them.

A minute or so later, the static echoes and repeats for Cianur.
Kur sees shadows of spirits; garbled, inhuman voices echo through his head.
He feels their haunting will linger.

Pilixa takes the worst of it.
Their rings instantly transmute into a heavy iron anvil.
Their hand is crushed by the sudden weight, demanding impromptu surgery on the spot.
If not for Kur applying his vet kit hours later, Pilixa might not have made it.
