## Post party

[Tam 'HR](../../directory/Atles/TamHR.md), [Ski](../../directory/Atles/Ski.md), [Minuit](../../directory/Atles/Minuit.md) and [Rise Ghost](../../directory/Atles/RiseGhost.md) are half a day away from the [Porcelain Citadel](../../directory/PorcelainCitadel/index.md) when night comes.
They set up camp and see, from a distance, coloured lights and tinny music drifting from the citadel.
They will miss the [Goodbye Party](ep_007.md).

The next morning, after the purple haze thins, they see the city of the Porcelain Princes, white and kiln-bright from afar.
The column of defence golems stands around it like patient teeth.
Four robed individuals cross the road and turn their faceless, glazed masks as one:

> "The stairs lead to the High Houses. Only Permitted Penitents may ascend to serve us there."

Then, in the same movement, they turn their faces ahead and continue their morning walk.

## A week in the citadel

The merchants enter the citadel and get to work.
Ski and Minuit spend a week on market research and trade.
They pack their beasts with Chitin Caps.
They hope to cash in at six times profit at the [Last Serai](../../directory/LastSerai/index.md).

During this time, Tam speaks with *Rainbowlanders* from the neighbourhood known as the Houses of Many Colours.
With them, she learns of an old mine of *Qasso* metal, west of the citadel, in the Locust Spires.
The place is supposedly very dangerous.

Rise Ghost approaches the week from a more artistic perspective.
He performs in the streets for money.
Most people show little interest, but the princes do.
A large group of porcelain-masked people follows the performance over the week, pale and attentive as moons.

By the end of the week, [Bone Kaolin](../../directory/PorcelainCitadel/BoneKaolin.md) gives two miniature porcelain masks to Rise Ghost:

> "The most valuable gifts from the princes."

So they say.

## Journey to the Last Serai

As the little (now a little less little) caravan leaves the citadel, they realise their trousers are ripped at the seat.
Something in the air seems to have nibbled away at the fabric.
So they say.

A few hours out, they encounter another porcelain prince.
This is a 10-body one.
Celadon introduces themselves and shows their collection of dryland coral from within their robes.
When Rise Ghost shows the miniature masks, Celadon immediately bows:

> "So Bone Kaolin has found their third body."

A few days later, the travellers develop a lung infection, possibly due to the purple haze at night.
Tam designs a mask made of her (or Minuit's) Dodo plumage.
The Dodo was not comfortable with the choice of material.
That night, Rise Ghost induces a womb-worthy high on Ski.

## Banana seller

A couple of days away from the Last Serai, they encounter a group of four protection-wired Necroamblers.
They work hard to protect their orange monk and the monk's little monk companion.
A banana seller approaches from the heat-hazed road.

As the three groups meet, the Necroamblers' stress grows:

> "You there, don't get close to Master."

The monk answers only with complaints, in an unintelligible language.
The banana seller continues:

> "Who wants bananas? The juiciest. The sweetest. Two bananas for €1. Bananas?"

Then the banana seller explodes.
His pieces fall to the ground.
A Necroambler picks one piece up:

> "This is no banana."
