Three days out, you sight it. A metallic stepped tower, glinting in
the daylight, glowing a ghostly, coppery green by night. Two days
out, you smell it. Soft and seductive like cocoa. A day out, you hear it.
Drumming out a rhythmless, rumbling staccato.

Closing in on the tower you see buildings, like hunched old men,
clustered in the lee of a cinder dune. Around the tower a circle of
gentle dust floats in a massive static charge. Nothing living grows
within that circle. There the Last Serai’s grand old harmonic rods
draw energy from that magical field, powering the great hold of the
Porcelain Princes while selling the excess to the last trading house
of the Violet City and the final embassy of the Spectrum Satraps.
