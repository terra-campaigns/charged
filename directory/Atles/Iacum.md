"Conjuring rain during drought, straightforward ritual. Ten of us, hired by the village, we only met that afternoon... drought, conjure rain, routine stuff... But the air, dense, resistant to movement, near tangible.

We looked up and there it was, swimming through the sky towards us, a cloud in the shape of a massive predator fish. Change incantation, the chant desperate as hints of lightning crackle across its body.

And then, the strike, violent, hitting the group. All dead, except me, the bolt passing through my body to ground.

I was revived an hour later in a landscape littered with fish scales. It had been raining scales since the strike. It was only now, as the downpour subsided that they felt safe approaching the ritual space.

They bid me goodbye the next day. Some thought me a god, hopeful of my return. Others saw a demon that they were glad to see leave."
